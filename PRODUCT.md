# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Restaurant owners, operators, and small hospitality teams using Biztro to create, edit, organize, and publish digital menus. They are usually working quickly between service, menu updates, and operational tasks, often on laptops but with mobile-first expectations.

## Product Purpose

Biztro helps hospitality businesses manage digital menus from draft to publication with less friction. Success means teams can update pricing, categories, translations, media, and publishing state confidently, without losing context or second-guessing what customers will see.

## Brand Personality

Clear, reliable, efficient. The interface should feel calm under pressure, operationally trustworthy, and fast to act on. It should support expert work without visual noise or decorative flourish competing with the task.

## Anti-references

Avoid crypto-style neon dashboards, over-gamified creator tools, and template SaaS admin panels that bury the task under marketing-like decoration. Avoid interfaces that feel playful when the user is trying to make precise menu updates before service.

## Design Principles

- Keep operators oriented: the current state, selected locale, and editable scope should always be obvious.
- Reduce hesitation: make destructive or irreversible decisions feel deliberate, but make routine editing feel immediate.
- Prioritize operational clarity over novelty: familiar patterns, explicit labels, and stable layout beat cleverness.
- Show the customer-facing impact: when editing menu content, help users understand what guests will actually read.
- Support rapid updates with confidence: validation, loading, and empty states should guide the next action instead of merely reporting status.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-accessible editing flows. Respect reduced-motion preferences, preserve clear focus indicators, and ensure translation and form workflows are understandable for users working quickly under time pressure.
